Consonant Sounds

Master the essential consonant sounds in English pronunciation

Stop Consonants

Stop consonants are made by completely blocking the air flow, then releasing it.

Common Stop Consonants:

  • /p/ and /b/ (lips together)
  • /t/ and /d/ (tongue behind teeth)
  • /k/ and /g/ (back of tongue)

Practice Words:

  • pat/bat, pin/bin, park/bark
  • tie/die, ten/den, tap/dap
  • cat/gap, key/gear, back/bag

Fricatives

Fricatives are made by forcing air through a narrow channel.

Common Fricatives:

  • /f/ and /v/ (lip and teeth)
  • /s/ and /z/ (tongue behind teeth)
  • /θ/ and /ð/ (th sounds)
  • /ʃ/ and /ʒ/ (sh and measure sounds)

Practice Words:

  • fan/van, fine/vine, safe/save
  • seal/zeal, rice/rise, face/phase
  • thin/this, thank/that, bath/bathe
  • ship/measure, sheet/genre, wash/garage

Other Consonants

These include nasals, liquids, and glides.

Types:

  • Nasals: /m/, /n/, /ŋ/
  • Liquids: /l/, /r/
  • Glides: /w/, /j/

Practice Words:

  • man, name, sing
  • light, right, clear
  • way, yes, water, youth
