Pronunciation Basics

Master the fundamental elements of English pronunciation

Word Stress

In English, certain syllables in words are stressed (pronounced with more emphasis). Understanding word stress is crucial for clear pronunciation.

Rules for Word Stress:

  • Two-syllable nouns often stress the first syllable (TABLE, APPLE)
  • Two-syllable verbs often stress the second syllable (preSENT, conTROL)
  • Words ending in -tion, -sion stress the second-to-last syllable (eduCAtion, teleVIsion)

Practice Words:

  • PREsent (noun) vs preSENT (verb)
  • REcord (noun) vs reCORD (verb)
  • OBject (noun) vs obJECT (verb)

Sentence Stress

In English sentences, content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are stressed more than function words (articles, prepositions, pronouns).

Key Points:

  • Content words carry the meaning and are stressed
  • Function words are usually unstressed
  • Stress helps create the rhythm of English speech

Example Sentences:

  • "I am going to the STORE" (stress on 'store')
  • "She LIKES to READ BOOKS" (stress on 'likes', 'read', 'books')
  • "The WEATHER is BEAUTIFUL today" (stress on 'weather', 'beautiful')

Intonation

Intonation is the rise and fall of voice pitch in speech. It helps convey meaning and emotion.

Common Patterns:

  • Falling intonation for statements
  • Rising intonation for yes/no questions
  • Falling-rising intonation for lists

Practice Sentences:

  • Statement: "I'm going home." (falling)
  • Question: "Are you ready?" (rising)
  • List: "I need apples, oranges, and bananas." (falling-rising)
