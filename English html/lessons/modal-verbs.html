Modal Verbs

Learn how to use modal verbs to express ability, permission, and possibility

Can and Could

These modal verbs are used to express:

  • Ability (physical or mental)
  • Permission
  • Possibility
  • Requests

Examples:

  • I can speak three languages. (ability)
  • Can I use your phone? (permission)
  • It could rain tomorrow. (possibility)

Must and Have to

These modal verbs express:

  • Obligation and necessity
  • Strong recommendations
  • Logical deduction

Examples:

  • You must wear a seatbelt. (obligation)
  • I have to finish this report today. (necessity)
  • She must be tired after working all day. (logical deduction)

Should and Ought to

Use these modal verbs for:

  • Advice and recommendations
  • Expectations
  • Correct or ideal situations

Examples:

  • You should exercise regularly. (advice)
  • The package should arrive tomorrow. (expectation)
  • Children ought to respect their elders. (ideal situation)
