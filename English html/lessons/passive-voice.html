The Passive Voice

Learn when and how to use passive voice in English

What is Passive Voice?

The passive voice is used when:

  • The action is more important than the doer
  • The doer is unknown or unimportant
  • We want to be formal or objective

Examples:

  • Active: Someone stole my bike.
  • Passive: My bike was stolen.

Forming the Passive Voice

To form the passive voice, we need:

  • The appropriate form of 'to be'
  • The past participle of the main verb
  • Optional: 'by + doer' at the end

Examples:

  • Active: They build houses.
  • Passive: Houses are built (by them).
  • Active: Shakespeare wrote this play.
  • Passive: This play was written by Shakespeare.

Common Uses of Passive Voice

Passive voice is commonly used in:

  • Scientific writing
  • News reports
  • Formal documents
  • When describing processes

Examples:

  • The experiment was conducted under strict conditions.
  • The new law will be passed next week.
  • English is spoken in many countries.
